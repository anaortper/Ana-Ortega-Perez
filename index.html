About

A scholar working across applied linguistics, education, and communication.

Ana Ortega Pérez is a Lecturer at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte. She holds a Ph.D. in Spanish from UC Davis, with a graduate program in Spanish Linguistics and a Designated Emphasis in Second Language Acquisition, and a Ph.D. in Communications from the Universidad de Sevilla.

Her academic trajectory brings together language education, journalism, media studies, intercultural communication, and community-engaged pedagogy. Her current work examines how language classrooms can support multilingual learners through critical language awareness, plain language, heritage language pedagogy, and human-centered uses of technology.

Research

Areas of inquiry

My work connects language education with questions of access, identity, literacy, community engagement, and pedagogical design.

Spanish heritage language education

Pedagogies that recognize bilingualism, identity, linguistic legitimacy, and the educational experiences of heritage speakers.

Critical Language Awareness

Classroom practices that make language, power, ideology, and linguistic hierarchies visible to learners.

Plain language

Accessible communication in Spanish and bilingual contexts, especially where institutional language shapes access to information.

AI literacy in language education

Ethical and reflective uses of AI in writing, feedback, and multilingual learning environments.

Graded readers & OER

Open materials that support reading, intercultural awareness, and community-centered curricular design.

Intercultural communicative competence

Instructional approaches that connect language, culture, social meaning, and learner experience.

Teaching

Inclusive and reflective language teaching.

My teaching experience includes first-year Spanish, intermediate composition, Spanish for heritage speakers, Spanish linguistics, Spanish for education, and Spanish for the professions. I design courses and materials that foreground communication, reflection, cultural awareness, and learner agency.

Heritage Spanish

SPA 31, SPA 32, SPA 33, and virtual heritage language course design.

Spanish language instruction

Elementary and intermediate Spanish across in-person, hybrid, virtual, and asynchronous formats.

Spanish linguistics & professional Spanish

The Structure of Spanish: Sounds & Words; Spanish for Education; Spanish for the Professions course design.

Community teaching

Volunteer course design and instruction for learners from preschool children to adult and retired learners.
